The easiest way to send crypto.

Peanuts Protocol let's you send tokens to anyone, with just a link. No need for their wallet address.

Make a deposit.

Select the amount you want to send, and approve the transaction. Your funds will be stored securely (and permissionlessly) in the smart contract. You'll get a link that you can share with anyone so they can claim the funds.

Recipient gets link.

Your beneficiary gets a link, and now can claim the funds. They can then either select to send the funds to an existing wallet address, or set up a new one on the spot where we'll send the funds to.

How this works.

You want to send crypto (Ethereum, or ERC-20 tokens, or ERC-721s) to someone. But you don't know their address. Or maybe they don't have one. Or maybe they hate crypto. Doesn't matter. You just need to know their email address, or their phone #, and we'll generate a link that you can share with them.

Let's walk through what happens, in order. The sender deposits an amount of tokens in the smart contract, and a corresponding passphrase is generated. A link containing this passphrase is created, and you can now share it with the recipient (or we can email it to them for you). Thanks to zk-technology, whoever has that link can also claim the funds deposited!

This makes it really easy to send money, or erc-20 tokens, or even erc-721s (NFTs)! Just deposit them in the smart contract, and give the appropriate link to whomever you want!
